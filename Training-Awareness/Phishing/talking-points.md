# Phishing - Key Talking Points

## What is Phishing?
- Social engineering attack impersonating trusted entities
- Goal: Steal credentials, install malware, or commit financial fraud
- Types: Email phishing, spear phishing, whaling, smishing, vishing

## Red Flags to Watch For
1. **Urgency/Pressure** - "Act now or lose access!"
2. **Suspicious sender address** - Look-alike domains (micr0soft.com)
3. **Generic greetings** - "Dear Customer" vs. your actual name
4. **Poor grammar/spelling** - Professional orgs proofread
5. **Unexpected attachments** - Invoices, shipping notices you didn't expect
6. **Mismatched URLs** - Hover to reveal real destination
7. **Requests for sensitive info** - Passwords, SSN, credit cards

## Action Steps (STOP-VERIFY-REPORT-DELETE)
1. **STOP** - Don't click links or open attachments
2. **VERIFY** - Contact sender through known-good channels
3. **REPORT** - Forward to IT security or use phishing report button
4. **DELETE** - Remove from inbox after reporting

## Key Statistics
- 91% of cyberattacks begin with phishing
- Average cost: $1.6M per incident
- 82 seconds: Average time to first click on phishing link
- 3.4 billion phishing emails sent daily worldwide

## Common Phishing Scenarios
- "Your password is expiring"
- "Unusual sign-in activity detected"
- "Your package couldn't be delivered"
- "Invoice attached" from unknown vendor
- "CEO requesting urgent wire transfer"

## Defense Strategies
- Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Use password managers
- Keep software updated
- Report suspicious emails
- When in doubt, verify through official channels

## Remember
> "Think before you click" - You are the first line of defense
