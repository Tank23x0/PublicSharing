# Data Protection Quiz

## Question 1
**What is the average cost of a data breach?**

A) $500,000  
B) $1.5 million  
C) $4.45 million  
D) $10 million

✅ **Correct Answer: C) $4.45 million**

---

## Question 2
**Which data classification requires the strictest controls?**

A) Public  
B) Internal  
C) Confidential  
D) Restricted/Highly Confidential

✅ **Correct Answer: D) Restricted/Highly Confidential**

---

## Question 3
**What should you do if you're unsure about a data classification?**

A) Assume it's public  
B) Treat it as confidential  
C) Delete it  
D) Share it anyway

✅ **Correct Answer: B) Treat it as confidential**

---

## Question 4
**Which of the following is considered PII (Personally Identifiable Information)?**

A) Company address  
B) Social Security Number  
C) Public marketing materials  
D) Published product prices

✅ **Correct Answer: B) Social Security Number**

---

## Question 5
**What is the "need-to-know" principle?**

A) You should know everything about the company  
B) You should only access data necessary for your job  
C) You need to know your password by heart  
D) You need to know your colleague's passwords

✅ **Correct Answer: B) You should only access data necessary for your job**

---

## Question 6
**You accidentally send a confidential file to the wrong email address. What should you do?**

A) Hope they delete it and say nothing  
B) Send a follow-up email asking them to delete it  
C) Report it to your manager and IT Security immediately  
D) Wait to see if anything bad happens

✅ **Correct Answer: C) Report it to your manager and IT Security immediately**

---

## Question 7
**Where should you store confidential company data?**

A) Your personal Dropbox  
B) Approved company storage locations only  
C) A USB drive for safekeeping  
D) Your personal email for backup

✅ **Correct Answer: B) Approved company storage locations only**

---

## Question 8
**How should you dispose of physical documents containing sensitive information?**

A) Regular trash  
B) Recycling bin  
C) Cross-cut shredding  
D) Just tear them up

✅ **Correct Answer: C) Cross-cut shredding**

---

## Question 9
**What is the maximum GDPR fine for serious data protection violations?**

A) $1 million  
B) $5 million  
C) $10 million  
D) €20 million or 4% of global annual revenue

✅ **Correct Answer: D) €20 million or 4% of global annual revenue**

---

## Question 10
**When should you report a potential data breach?**

A) After you've investigated it yourself  
B) Only if you're certain data was stolen  
C) Immediately, even if you're not sure of the extent  
D) At the end of the week in your status report

✅ **Correct Answer: C) Immediately, even if you're not sure of the extent**
