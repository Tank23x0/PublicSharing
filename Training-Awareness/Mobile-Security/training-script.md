# Mobile Device Security Training Script

> **Duration:** ~5 minutes | **Target Audience:** All employees using mobile devices for work

---

## [INTRO - 0:00-0:30]

**[NARRATOR]**

Your smartphone knows everything about you—your location, your contacts, your passwords, your conversations. And increasingly, it also has access to your work email, company documents, and business applications.

This combination of personal and professional makes mobile security more important than ever.

---

## [SECTION 1: The Mobile Security Threat - 0:30-1:15]

**[NARRATOR]**

Mobile devices face unique security challenges.

**[ON SCREEN: Threat landscape]**

**Why Attackers Target Mobile:**
- Always connected, always on
- Mix of personal and work data
- Users less suspicious on mobile
- Smaller screens make phishing harder to spot
- Apps may have excessive permissions

**Mobile-Specific Threats:**
- Smishing (SMS phishing)
- Malicious apps
- Lost or stolen devices
- Unsecured WiFi connections
- QR code attacks (quishing)

---

## [SECTION 2: Device Protection Basics - 1:15-2:15]

**[NARRATOR]**

Start with these fundamental protections.

**[ON SCREEN: Device security checklist]**

**Screen Lock**
- Use a strong PIN (at least 6 digits) or complex password
- Enable biometrics (fingerprint, face recognition)
- Set auto-lock to 1-2 minutes maximum
- Never share your unlock code

**Software Updates**
- Enable automatic updates
- Update apps regularly
- Don't ignore security patches
- Replace devices that no longer receive updates

**Find My Device**
- Enable "Find My iPhone" or "Find My Device" (Android)
- Know how to remotely locate, lock, and wipe
- Test these features before you need them

**Encryption**
- Most modern devices are encrypted by default
- Ensure encryption is enabled
- Use strong screen lock to protect encryption keys

---

## [SECTION 3: App Security - 2:15-3:00]

**[NARRATOR]**

Apps are the gateway to your device—and your data.

**[ON SCREEN: App security guidelines]**

**Installing Apps**
- Only download from official stores (App Store, Google Play)
- Research apps before installing—check reviews and ratings
- Be suspicious of apps asking for excessive permissions
- Avoid "jailbreaking" or "rooting" your device

**App Permissions**
- Review what permissions apps request
- Does a flashlight need access to your contacts? No.
- Revoke unnecessary permissions
- Regularly audit which apps have which access

**Work Apps**
- Use only company-approved apps for work
- Keep work and personal apps separate when possible
- Follow Mobile Device Management (MDM) requirements

---

## [SECTION 4: Connecting Safely - 3:00-3:45]

**[NARRATOR]**

How you connect matters as much as what you connect to.

**[ON SCREEN: Secure connections]**

**WiFi Safety**
- Avoid connecting to unknown WiFi networks
- Use VPN when on public WiFi
- Verify network names before connecting (attackers create lookalikes)
- Disable auto-connect to open networks

**Bluetooth**
- Turn off Bluetooth when not in use
- Don't pair with unknown devices
- Use "non-discoverable" mode when possible

**Charging**
- Avoid public USB charging stations ("juice jacking" risk)
- Use your own charger and power adapter
- Consider USB data blockers if you must use public ports

---

## [SECTION 5: If Your Device is Lost or Stolen - 3:45-4:30]

**[NARRATOR]**

Act immediately if your device is lost or stolen.

**[ON SCREEN: Emergency steps]**

**Immediately:**
1. Use "Find My Device" to locate it
2. If unrecoverable, remotely lock it
3. If sensitive data is at risk, remotely wipe it
4. Report to IT Security immediately
5. Change passwords for accounts accessed on the device

**Prevention:**
- Never leave devices unattended
- Don't keep them visible in parked cars
- Be aware of your surroundings in public
- Keep track of devices during travel

---

## [OUTRO - 4:30-5:00]

**[NARRATOR]**

Your mobile device is a powerful tool—and a significant security responsibility. Treat it with the same care you'd give a laptop containing all your sensitive information, because that's exactly what it is.

Remember:
- Strong locks and current software
- Apps from trusted sources only
- Connect securely, especially on public networks
- Report lost devices immediately

Stay mobile, stay secure.
