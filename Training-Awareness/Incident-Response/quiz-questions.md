# Incident Response Quiz

## Question 1
**What should you do FIRST if you suspect a malware infection on your computer?**

A) Turn off the computer immediately  
B) Try to remove the malware yourself  
C) Disconnect from the network and report to IT Security  
D) Continue working and report at the end of the day

✅ **Correct Answer: C) Disconnect from the network and report to IT Security**

---

## Question 2
**Why should you NOT turn off a computer suspected of being compromised?**

A) It might damage the hard drive  
B) Forensic evidence in memory will be lost  
C) The malware might spread faster  
D) IT needs it on to remote in

✅ **Correct Answer: B) Forensic evidence in memory will be lost**

---

## Question 3
**When should you report a potential security incident?**

A) After you've investigated it yourself  
B) Only if you're certain it's a real incident  
C) Immediately, even if you're not sure  
D) At your next scheduled meeting with your manager

✅ **Correct Answer: C) Immediately, even if you're not sure**

---

## Question 4
**You accidentally sent a spreadsheet with customer data to the wrong email address. This is:**

A) Not a security incident - just a mistake  
B) A security incident that should be reported  
C) Something to ignore since the email can't be recalled  
D) A problem only if the recipient complains

✅ **Correct Answer: B) A security incident that should be reported**

---

## Question 5
**What information should you document when reporting an incident?**

A) Only your employee ID  
B) Only the time it happened  
C) What happened, when, what you were doing, and any error messages  
D) Just forward the suspicious email

✅ **Correct Answer: C) What happened, when, what you were doing, and any error messages**

---

## Question 6
**You clicked on a link in a suspicious email but nothing seemed to happen. What should you do?**

A) Assume you're fine since nothing visible happened  
B) Run your own antivirus scan and continue working  
C) Report it to IT Security—malware can run invisibly  
D) Delete the email and forget about it

✅ **Correct Answer: C) Report it to IT Security—malware can run invisibly**

---

## Question 7
**Who can you discuss an active security incident with?**

A) Anyone in the company  
B) Only those directly involved and IT Security (need-to-know basis)  
C) Your family so they can watch out too  
D) Everyone via company-wide email to warn them

✅ **Correct Answer: B) Only those directly involved and IT Security (need-to-know basis)**

---

## Question 8
**Your laptop was stolen from your car. When should you report this?**

A) When you've finished filing a police report  
B) After you've searched everywhere it might be  
C) Immediately  
D) When you return to the office

✅ **Correct Answer: C) Immediately**

---

## Question 9
**How does early reporting help during a security incident?**

A) It doesn't make much difference  
B) It reduces damage and costs by enabling faster response  
C) It just creates more paperwork  
D) It's only important for compliance reasons

✅ **Correct Answer: B) It reduces damage and costs by enabling faster response**

---

## Question 10
**Which of these is an example of a security incident?**

A) Your password is about to expire  
B) A colleague asks for help with their computer  
C) You notice unauthorized charges on your personal credit card  
D) You receive a phishing email (even if you didn't click it)

✅ **Correct Answer: D) You receive a phishing email (even if you didn't click it)**
