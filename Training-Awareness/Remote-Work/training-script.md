# Remote Work Security Training Script

> **Duration:** ~5 minutes | **Target Audience:** All remote/hybrid employees

---

## [INTRO - 0:00-0:30]

**[NARRATOR]**

Working from home offers flexibility and convenience, but it also creates new security challenges. Your home is now an extension of the office—and that means office-level security standards apply.

Let's learn how to stay secure while working remotely.

---

## [SECTION 1: Secure Your Home Network - 0:30-1:30]

**[NARRATOR]**

Your home network is now a corporate network entry point.

**[ON SCREEN: Network security checklist]**

**WiFi Security**
- Use WPA3 or WPA2 encryption—never WEP
- Change your router's default password
- Create a strong WiFi password (not your address or phone number)
- Consider a separate network for work devices

**Router Security**
- Update router firmware regularly
- Disable remote management
- Change the default admin username
- Enable your router's firewall

**Public WiFi Warning**
- Avoid working on public WiFi when possible
- If you must use public WiFi, always use your VPN
- Never access sensitive data on unsecured networks

---

## [SECTION 2: Protect Your Devices - 1:30-2:30]

**[NARRATOR]**

Your work devices need extra protection at home.

**[ON SCREEN: Device security practices]**

**Keep Devices Updated**
- Enable automatic updates for OS and applications
- Don't postpone security updates
- Restart your computer regularly to apply updates

**Physical Security**
- Don't leave devices unattended in shared spaces
- Lock your screen when stepping away (Windows+L or Cmd+Q)
- Store devices securely when not in use
- Be aware of who can see your screen

**Work vs. Personal Separation**
- Use work devices for work only
- Don't let family members use work equipment
- Avoid storing personal files on work devices
- Keep work and personal accounts separate

---

## [SECTION 3: VPN and Secure Connections - 2:30-3:15]

**[NARRATOR]**

A VPN creates a secure tunnel for your work.

**[ON SCREEN: VPN explanation graphic]**

**VPN Best Practices**
- Connect to VPN before accessing company resources
- Keep VPN connected throughout your work session
- Only use the company-approved VPN
- Report VPN connection issues to IT

**Secure Communications**
- Use company-approved tools for work communication
- Don't discuss confidential information on personal apps
- Verify video call participants before sharing sensitive info
- Be cautious of screen sharing what's visible

---

## [SECTION 4: Your Home Office Environment - 3:15-4:00]

**[NARRATOR]**

Your physical environment affects your digital security.

**[ON SCREEN: Home office security]**

**Prevent Shoulder Surfing**
- Position your monitor away from windows
- Be aware of who can see your screen during video calls
- Use privacy screens if you work in shared spaces

**Audio Privacy**
- Use headphones for confidential calls
- Find a private space for sensitive discussions
- Mute when not speaking to avoid background leaks
- Be mindful of smart speakers and assistants

**Document Security**
- Shred sensitive printouts—don't just trash them
- Lock up physical documents when not in use
- Return confidential documents to the office

---

## [SECTION 5: Staying Vigilant at Home - 4:00-4:30]

**[NARRATOR]**

Working from home can make us less vigilant. Stay alert.

**[ON SCREEN: Remote work threats]**

**Increased Phishing Risk**
- Attackers target remote workers with fake IT support emails
- Verify requests through known channels
- Be suspicious of urgent requests changing payment details

**Social Engineering**
- Home addresses and routines may be researched
- Be careful what you share on social media
- Verify unexpected visitors or calls

**Incident Response**
- Report incidents immediately—even from home
- Keep IT Security contact info handy
- Know how to disconnect quickly if needed

---

## [OUTRO - 4:30-5:00]

**[NARRATOR]**

Remote work doesn't mean remote security. The same standards apply whether you're in the office or at your kitchen table.

Remember:
- Secure your network and devices
- Use VPN for all work activities
- Maintain physical and audio privacy
- Stay vigilant against phishing and social engineering

Your home office is now part of our security perimeter. Thank you for protecting it.

**[ON SCREEN: Company logo, "IT Security is here to help. Reach out anytime."]**
