# Compliance Basics - Key Talking Points

## Why Compliance Matters
- Protects customers, patients, and partners
- Avoids significant financial penalties
- Maintains business relationships
- Preserves reputation and trust
- May be legally required for your industry

## Major Regulations

### HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)
**Applies to:** Healthcare organizations, business associates
**Protects:** Protected Health Information (PHI)

**Key Requirements:**
- Minimum necessary access to PHI
- Secure transmission and storage
- Patient right to access their records
- Breach notification within 60 days
- Employee training requirements

### PCI-DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard)
**Applies to:** Anyone processing, storing, or transmitting credit card data
**Protects:** Cardholder data

**Key Requirements:**
- Never store CVV/CVC codes
- Encrypt cardholder data in transit and at rest
- Restrict access on need-to-know basis
- Regular security testing
- Maintain security policies

### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
**Applies to:** Any organization handling EU resident data
**Protects:** Personal data of EU residents

**Key Requirements:**
- Lawful basis for processing (consent, contract, etc.)
- Data minimization (only collect what you need)
- Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
- Breach notification within 72 hours
- Data Protection Impact Assessments

### SOC 2 (Service Organization Control 2)
**Applies to:** Service providers storing customer data in cloud
**Demonstrates:** Security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, privacy

**Key Requirements:**
- Security policies and procedures
- Access controls
- Encryption standards
- Incident response procedures
- Annual audits

## Your Daily Compliance Responsibilities

### Access Control
- Only access data needed for your job
- Never share credentials
- Lock workstation when away
- Report unauthorized access attempts

### Data Handling
- Know what type of data you're handling
- Use approved storage locations
- Follow retention schedules
- Dispose of data properly

### Documentation
- Follow documented procedures
- Complete training on time
- Maintain accurate records
- Document exceptions and approvals

### Reporting
- Report suspected violations immediately
- Don't try to fix compliance issues alone
- Cooperate with audits
- Ask questions when unsure

## Red Flags to Report
- Requests to bypass security controls
- Unauthorized access to sensitive data
- Missing audit logs or records
- Pressure to skip compliance steps
- Suspicious data requests

## Key Principle
> "Compliance protects people. Every regulation exists because someone was harmed without it."
